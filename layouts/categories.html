Bank Categories

1) Number of Branch locations (as compared to average)

The amount of locations the bank has.

2) Branch locations: distribution (as compared to average in low-moderate income areas)

The distance of the branches of the bank from each other.

3) Online Banking(as compared to average)

Doing all banking on the website of the bank.

4) Fee for Outside ATMs

Amount of money charged for using ATMs not owned by the bank.

5) Checking Account Fees (for the basic level and checkless checking account)

How much you have to pay to open a checking account or when you cannot keep an average balance specified by the bank.

6) Savings Account Fees (for the basic level account)

The amount for the monthly maintenance fee.

7) Overdraft practices

What happens when you withdraw more than what you have in your account.

8) Overdraft Thresholds and Limits

The maximum amount the bank will lend you when your account goes into overdraft.

9) Credit cards (basic secured and unsecured) (as compared to average APR range)

The annual amount charged by borrowing money (spending more money than you can pay off in a month and paying installments) on your credit card.

10) Credit Card Fees (Including late charges)

The annual fee credit card companies charge you each year to use the credit card.

11) Loan Origination marketshare (# of total $ volume of lending in state)

The amount of money loaned out in New York.

12) Loan acceptance rate in NY State (as compared to average)

The acceptance rate for accepting your loan and giving you money.

13) Loan acceptance rate Latino borrowers (as compared to average)

The acceptance rate for accepting your loan and giving you money if you are Latino.

14) Loan acceptance rate Black borrowers (same as above)

The acceptance rate for accepting your loan and giving you money if you are Black.

15) Loan acceptance rate low-moderate income borrowers (same as above)

The acceptance rate for accepting your loan and giving you money if you have a low to moderate income.

16) Percentage of total to Low- and Moderate-Income borrowers (as compared to average)

A comparison of the total amount of borrowers to the amount of low and moderate income borrowers.

17) Loan acceptance rate low-moderate-income communities (as compared to average)

The acceptance rate for accepting your loan and giving you money if you live in a low to moderate income community.

18) Percentage of total in communities of color (as compared to average)

The acceptance rate for accepting your loan and giving you money if you live in a community of color.

19) Acceptance of alternate forms of identification

If the bank will accept something other than the regular forms of identification to tell if you are who you say you are (e.g. a credit card bill instead of a passport).

20) Cost of wire transfers

The amount of money the bank will charge you to move money from your account to another person's account.
